# Diabetes Risk Analysis & Prevention Strategies

##  Overview
This project analyzes a large healthcare dataset to identify key risk factors contributing to diabetes. The goal is to uncover patterns in medical and socioeconomic variables to support early detection and prevention strategies.

---

##  Dataset
- Dataset size: 250,000+ records  
- Includes features such as:
  - BMI  
  - Blood Pressure  
  - Cholesterol  
  - Lifestyle factors  
  - Income and Education levels  

---

##  Tools & Technologies
- Python  
- Pandas, NumPy  
- Matplotlib, Seaborn  

---

##  Key Analysis Performed
- Data cleaning and preprocessing  
- Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)  
- Statistical analysis of health indicators  
- Visualization of trends and correlations  

---

##  Key Insights
- ~75% of diabetic individuals have high blood pressure  
- ~67% of diabetic individuals have high cholesterol  
- Higher BMI strongly correlates with increased diabetes risk  
- Lower income and education levels are associated with higher diabetes prevalence  

---

##  Business Impact
- Helps identify high-risk population segments  
- Supports data-driven healthcare decision-making  
- Provides insights for preventive healthcare strategies  

---

##  Project Structure
- `notebook.ipynb` → Data analysis and visualizations  
- `dataset.csv` → Source dataset
